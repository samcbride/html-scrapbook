A Journey to Vancouver

Vancouver, British Columbia

A Brief Description and History

Vancouver is located in British Columbia, the most westerly province in Canada. Vancouver is consistently named one of the top cities for liveability and quality of life, however it is also one of the most expensive cities and ranks as the most expensive city to live in Canada and the fourth-most expensive globally.

Ecology and Climate

Vancouver is one of Canada's warmest cities in winter and one of the coolest in summer compared to all other major metropolitan cities. Vancouver was a temperate rain forest due to its vegetation though many trees and plants that grow there today were imported from other areas. One of the most common is the Japanese cherry tree, which lines many streets throughout the city. They bloom in early spring each year and are celebrated by the Vancouver Cherry Blossom Festival.

Things To Do

There are many activities to do in Vancouver, such as:

  • Kayaking
  • Hiking
  • Swimming
  • Skiing

Things You Might Not Know

  1. Vancouver is often referred to colloquially as Hollywood North.
  2. Vancouver has a Shakespear Festival every summer called Bard on the Beach.
  3. Vancouver's Chinatown is Canada's largest Chinatown.
